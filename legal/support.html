2. Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

What is RianTuber and what does it do?

RianTuber is an iOS app designed to help you batch-upload videos from your device to YouTube. Instead of uploading videos one at a time through the YouTube app, RianTuber lets you select multiple videos, configure their titles, descriptions, tags, privacy settings, and playlists all at once, then upload them in a single batch. This saves significant time for content creators who regularly upload multiple videos.

How do I connect my YouTube account?

Tap the "Sign In" button on the main screen and follow the Google Sign-In flow. RianTuber uses Google's secure OAuth 2.0 authentication ‚Äî your Google password is never stored in the app. You will be asked to grant permission for RianTuber to upload videos and read basic channel information. You can revoke this access at any time.

Can I connect multiple YouTube channels?

RianTuber supports signing in with one Google account at a time. If your Google account has access to multiple YouTube channels (e.g., through Brand Accounts), you can select which channel to upload to within the app. To switch to a different Google account entirely, sign out and sign in with the other account.

Uploading Videos

Why does RianTuber need access to my Photos library?

RianTuber needs Photos library access to let you select videos for upload. When prompted, you can choose to grant access to your entire library or only selected photos and videos. The app only reads the specific videos you choose ‚Äî it does not scan, index, or catalog your entire library in the background.

My upload failed. What should I do?

Upload failures can occur for several reasons. Try the following steps:

  • Check your internet connection. Wi-Fi is recommended for large files. Unstable connections can cause uploads to fail mid-transfer.
  • Verify your YouTube account status. Ensure your account is in good standing and has not been suspended or restricted.
  • Check video requirements. Make sure your video meets YouTube's upload requirements for file size, duration, and format.
  • Retry the upload. RianTuber supports resumable uploads, so a failed upload can often be retried without starting from scratch.
  • Check YouTube API quota. YouTube enforces daily API quotas. If you've uploaded many videos in a short period, you may need to wait until the quota resets (typically at midnight Pacific Time).
  • Restart the app. Force-close RianTuber and reopen it, then retry the upload.

If the issue persists, contact us at support@riantuber.com with the error details.

Is there a limit on how many videos I can upload?

RianTuber itself does not impose upload limits. However, YouTube enforces daily API quotas and upload limits on your channel. New or unverified YouTube channels may have stricter limits (e.g., maximum video length of 15 minutes). To increase your limits, verify your YouTube channel at youtube.com/verify. For details on YouTube's upload limits, visit YouTube Help.

Can I schedule videos to be published at a specific time?

Yes. When configuring your upload, you can set a scheduled publish date and time. The video will be uploaded as "Private" and automatically published at the scheduled time by YouTube. Note that scheduling requires your YouTube channel to be in good standing.

What video formats does RianTuber support?

RianTuber supports all video formats that are stored in your iOS Photos library and accepted by YouTube, including MP4, MOV, and M4V. The app reads videos directly from your Photos library, so any video that plays on your device should be uploadable. For a full list of YouTube-supported formats, see YouTube's supported file formats.

Privacy and Security

Does RianTuber store my videos or personal data on a server?

No. RianTuber is a client-side app that operates entirely on your device. Your videos are uploaded directly from your device to YouTube's servers. We do not operate any backend servers that store, process, or have access to your videos, personal data, or account credentials. All app data (settings, upload history, cached channel info) is stored locally on your device and protected by iOS's built-in encryption.

How do I disconnect RianTuber from my Google account?

You can disconnect RianTuber from your Google account in two ways:

  • Within the App: Go to Settings and tap "Sign Out" to clear your session and cached data.
  • Via Google: Visit Google Account Permissions, find RianTuber in the list, and click "Remove Access." This immediately revokes all permissions granted to the app.
How do I delete all my data from RianTuber?

Since all data is stored locally on your device, you can delete it completely by:

  1. Signing out of your Google account within the App (this clears OAuth tokens and cached channel data)
  2. Uninstalling RianTuber from your device (this removes all remaining local data, including settings and upload history)
  3. Optionally, revoking access at Google Account Permissions to ensure the OAuth authorization is fully revoked

To remove videos that have already been uploaded to YouTube, manage them directly in YouTube Studio. If you need further assistance with data deletion, contact us at support@riantuber.com.

Compatibility and Technical

What iOS version does RianTuber require?

RianTuber requires iOS 16.0 or later. The app is compatible with iPhone and iPad. For the best experience, we recommend keeping your device updated to the latest iOS version.

Does RianTuber work on iPad?

Yes, RianTuber is compatible with iPad running iPadOS 16.0 or later. The interface adapts to the larger screen for a comfortable experience.

Why is my upload very slow?

Upload speed depends primarily on your internet connection. Large video files (especially 4K or long-duration videos) require significant bandwidth. To improve upload speed:

  • Use a stable Wi-Fi connection rather than cellular data
  • Avoid uploading during peak network usage times
  • Close other apps that may be using bandwidth
  • Move closer to your Wi-Fi router for a stronger signal
  • Consider reducing video resolution if upload speed is a persistent issue

3. Troubleshooting Guide

üîê "Sign-In Failed" or Authentication Errors

If you cannot sign in to your Google account:

  1. Ensure you have a stable internet connection
  2. Check that your Google account is not locked or suspended
  3. Go to Google Account Permissions and remove RianTuber, then try signing in again
  4. Restart the app and your device
  5. Update RianTuber to the latest version from the App Store
  6. If using a managed Google Workspace account, check with your administrator that third-party app access is enabled

üì§ Uploads Stuck at 0% or Not Starting

If uploads are not progressing:

  1. Check your internet connection ‚Äî try loading a webpage to confirm connectivity
  2. Ensure RianTuber has permission to access your Photos library (Settings ‚Üí RianTuber ‚Üí Photos)
  3. Verify that the video file is not corrupted ‚Äî try playing it in the Photos app
  4. Force-close the app and reopen it
  5. Check if YouTube is experiencing service disruptions at Google Workspace Status Dashboard

‚ö†Ô∏è "Quota Exceeded" Error

YouTube enforces daily API quotas that limit the number of API calls an app can make:

  1. Wait until the quota resets (typically at midnight Pacific Time / 8:00 AM UTC)
  2. Reduce the number of videos you upload in a single day
  3. Avoid repeatedly retrying failed uploads in quick succession, as each attempt consumes quota

  2. Ensure your device has sufficient free storage space
  3. Restart your device
  4. If the issue persists, uninstall and reinstall the app (note: this will clear local settings)
  5. Report the issue to support@riantuber.com with your device model, iOS version, and steps to reproduce

üîÑ Videos Not Appearing on YouTube After Upload

If your upload completed but the video is not visible:

  1. YouTube may still be processing the video ‚Äî this can take minutes to hours depending on video length and resolution
  2. Check YouTube Studio for the video's processing status
  3. Verify the privacy setting ‚Äî if set to "Private," the video won't be publicly visible
  4. If scheduled, the video will appear at the scheduled publish time
  5. Check your YouTube channel for any policy violations that may have caused the video to be removed
